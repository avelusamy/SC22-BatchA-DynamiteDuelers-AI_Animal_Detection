Our AI System 


   
   
 Predictions of tigers, birds, and bears.
    
     What does our system do?

      
      Choose the upload button to upload a file. Using this file, we will identify what animal
      is in the image, and the probability of our prediction being correct. This probability
      is also known as a confidence levels. Next to the image, we will also display the
      endangerment status of the animal that we have matched it to. Our list of accepted
      animals is as follows:
      
Alligators, Bears, Cats, Cows, Crocodiles, Dogs, Elephants, Horses, Lions, Lizards, Monkeys

      Pandas, Pigs, Sheep, Zebras, Birds, and Tigers
    

    
Our system does this by asking you to upload an image. This image can be of any one or more of 
the above animals. The AI system will run it through the database and look for a match. It will then
return the name of the animal, and how confident it is. Then, the AI will use its predictions to look 
an endangerment status match. All of the results will be displayed to you in a matter of seconds!
         
Sometimes, however, the image prediction may be wrong. This could be because the AI system got 
confused on what animal it was. Or, the AI system did not have data on that animal. Another reason 
that this could occur is because there wasn't enough data on a certain animal. Though we tried 
our best to have equal data distribution, there are still some flaws.

Resources 




Here are some of the resources that we used 
throughout the creation of this project:




We utilized CoCalc for the majority of our programming and training the AI. 
We used both CPU and GPU servers while making this project.




When our Cocalc servers were down, we used Google Colab for additional training 
for the model.




To gather some of the images for our dataset, we used pre-made 
datasets that we found on Kaggle.




In addition to Kaggle, our team used SerpApi to web-scrape additional 
images for the dataset.




Roboflow was used to compile all of our images and finalize the 
multiple versions of our dataset.




Weights and Biases was used to evaluate the model during training by 
generating graphs and matrices to display the model's performance.




  



  

    

      Process
    

  


  
  

  
    

      1)Find twenty species of animal using images from Kaggle and web
      scraping.
      

      ->
      

      2)Use Roboflow to annotate and label the species of animals in the image.
      

      ->
      

      3) We utilized Cocalc for our programming of the AI to identify animals,
      

      using CPU and GPU servers.
      

      ->
      

      4) Using Weights&Biases, we tested our images, and took note of
      roadblocks, such as
      

      confusion between alligators and crocodiles, on confusion matrices.
      

      ->
      

      5) Using the data, we eliminated potential roadblocks, such as the
      alligator and crocodile
      

      identification and improved increasing confidence in identifying other
      species of animals.
      

      ->
      

      6) We then fixed the roadblocks, and improved our AI to identify animals
      in an image
      

      with increased accuracy and more confidence.
    



    

    

      

        

        

          

            Challenges
          


          


          

            Our Challenges and Solutions
          


          

            Challenge: Our data sets were not giving us precise results
            

            Solution: We modified our data set in Roboflow by adding/deleting
            images, and
            

            managing the classes
            

            Challenge: Yolov5 confusing Alligators and Crocodiles
            

            Solution: We added more data to the
            

            Alligators data set, and removed the Crocodile data set
            

            Challenge: Our Colab and Cocalc servers were crashing very quickly
            

            Solution: We asked our instructor for help, and decreased the size
            of our data set
